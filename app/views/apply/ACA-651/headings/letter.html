Dear {{ dpName }}

Jane Doe has applied for Carer’s Allowance from {{ claimDate }} and named you as the person they care for.

This is a benefit for carers who look after someone for 35 hours a week or more.

What this means for you

You do not need to do anything unless Jane Doe is not caring for you. You can contact us to report this.

We will need to check your benefit records to deal with Jane Doe’s claim. You can find out how we use your data at:
www.gov.uk/dwp/personal‑information‑charter

If your benefits include a severe disability premium

If the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid, you will stop getting any severe disability premium.

A severe disability premium is an extra amount of money that may be added to your:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Housing Benefit

If you get Pension Credit

If you are paid an extra amount of Pension Credit for severe disability, this will stop if the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid.

If you pay a reduced rate of Council Tax

Your Council Tax reduction could be affected if the Carer’s Allowance claim is successful. You can check this with your local council at:
www.gov.uk/find-local-council

If you need to speak to someone about your benefits

Contact the department that deals with your benefit if you need to check whether Carer’s Allowance will affect your payments.

If you need to contact us about Carer’s Allowance

  • Telephone: 0800 731 0297

You can find out more about ways to contact us at:
