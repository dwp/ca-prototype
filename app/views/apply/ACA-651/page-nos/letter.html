A new claim for Carer’s Allowance

If you need this letter in a different format, for example, Braille, large print or audio, contact us by telephone on 0800 587 0912 or textphone on 0800 012 1574.

Dear ((dp_name))

((carer_name)) has applied for Carer’s Allowance from ((claim_date)) and named you as the person they care for.

This is a benefit for carers who look after someone for 35 hours a week or more.

What this means for you

You do not need to do anything unless ((carer_name)) is not caring for you. You can contact us to report this.

We will need to check your benefit records to deal with the Carer’s Allowance claim. You can find out how we use your data at:
www.communities-ni.gov.uk/dfc-information-charter

If your benefit includes a severe disability premium

If the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid, the rate of severe disability premium you get will either stop or go down.

A severe disability premium is an extra amount of money that may be added to your:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Housing Benefit

If you get an extra amount of Pension Credit for severe disability

If the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid, the extra amount you get for severe disability will either stop or go down.

If you get rates relief

The rates you pay could be affected if the Carer’s Allowance claim is successful. You can check this with the Land & Property Services:
www.nidirect.gov.uk/land-property-services

If you need to speak to someone about your benefits

Contact the office that deals with your benefit if you need to check whether Carer’s Allowance will affect your payments.

If you need to contact us about Carer’s Allowance

You can contact us for free on:

  • Telephone: 0800 587 0912
  • Textphone: 0800 012 1574

You can find out more about ways to contact us at:
www.nidirect.gov.uk/contacts/disability-and-carers-service

If you need to speak to someone about your benefits, contact the department that deals with the benefit.

Yours sincerely

The Disability and Carers Service
