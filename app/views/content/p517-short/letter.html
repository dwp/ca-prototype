A new claim for Carer’s Allowance

Jane Doe has applied for Carer’s Allowance from 28 September 2020 and named you as the person they care for.

What this means for you

You do not need to do anything unless Jane Doe is not caring for you. You can contact us to report this.

We will need to access your benefit records to deal with Jane Doe’s claim. We will not share any information about your benefits with them.

If the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid, you will stop getting any severe disability premium you are paid as part of your benefits.

If you get an extra amount of Pension Credit for severe disability, or pay reduced council tax, these benefits could be also affected.

Contact the department that deals with your benefit if you need to check whether Carer’s Allowance will affect your payments.

If you need to contact us

You can contact us about Carer’s Allowance for free on:

  • Telephone: 0800 731 0297
  • Textphone: 0800 731 0317
  • Relay UK: 18001 then 0800 731 0297 (text service if you cannot hear or speak on the phone)

You can find out more about ways to contact us at www.gov.uk/carers-allowance-unit.

If you need to speak to someone about your benefits, contact the department that deals with the benefit.

If you need this letter in a different format

If you need this letter in a more accessible format, for example large print or Braille, contact us by phone on the number above.
