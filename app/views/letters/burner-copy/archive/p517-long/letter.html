A new claim for Carer’s Allowance

Dear {{ dpName }}

{{ carerName }} has applied for Carer’s Allowance from {{ claimDate }} and named you as the person they care for.

This is a benefit for carers who look after someone for 35 hours a week or more.

What this means for you

You do not need to do anything unless {{ carerName }} is not caring for you. You can contact us to report this.

We will need to access your benefit records to deal with {{ carerName }}’s claim.

If your benefits include a severe disability premium

If the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid, you will stop getting any severe disability premium.

A severe disability premium is an extra amount of money that may be added to your:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Housing Benefit

If you get Pension Credit

If you are paid an extra amount of Pension Credit for severe disability, this will stop if the Carer’s Allowance claim is paid.

If you pay a reduced rate of council tax

Your council tax reduction could be affected if the Carer’s Allowance claim is successful. You can check this with your local council at www.gov.uk/find-local-council.

If you need to contact us

You can contact us about Carer’s Allowance for free on:

  • Telephone: 0800 731 0297
  • Textphone: 0800 731 0317
  • Relay UK: 18001 then 0800 731 0297 (text service if you cannot hear or speak on the phone)

You can find out more about ways to contact us at www.gov.uk/carers-allowance-unit.

If you need to speak to someone about your benefits, contact the department that deals with the benefit.

If you need this letter in a different format, for example, Braille, large print or audio, contact us by telephone on 0800 731 0297 or textphone on 0800 731 0317.

Yours sincerely

The Carer's Allowance Unit
