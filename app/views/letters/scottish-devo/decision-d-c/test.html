Dear {{ carerName }}

I am writing to tell you that we cannot pay you Carer's Allowance from [Insert Date] to [Insert Date]. This is because you are not entitled to Carer's Allowance because the person you are looking after does or did not get an appropriate disability benefit.

The law says that a person may be entitled to Carer’s Allowance for the week if they are regularly and substantially engaged in caring for a severely disabled person.

A severely disabled person is someone who receives a qualifying benefit or a qualifying benefit is paid to someone else as an appointee, parent or guardian. A qualifying benefit for Carer’s Allowance purposes is one of the following:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Disability Living Allowance care component at the middle or highest rate
  • Constant Attendance Allowance at the full day rate or more
  • Personal Independence payment daily living component
  • Armed Forces Independence Payment

Social Security Contributions and Benefits Act 1992, section 70(2)
Social Security (Invalid Care Allowance) Regulations 1976, regulation 3

What to do if you think this decision is wrong

If you think the decision is wrong, please get in touch with us by telephone or in writing, within one month of the date of this letter. If you do not contact us within one month of the date of this letter we may only be able to change the decision from the date you contact us. Our telephone number and address are on the front page of this letter.

You can appeal against this decision, but you cannot appeal until we have looked at the decision again. We call this a Mandatory Reconsideration.

You, or someone who has the authority to act for you, can:

  • ask us for an explanation of the decision, or
  • ask for a written statement of reasons for the decision, if we have not already sent one
  • ask us to look at the decision again, to see if it can be changed. There may be some facts you think we have overlooked, or you may have further information that affects the decision.

When we have looked at the decision again, we will send you a letter explaining what we have done. We call this a Mandatory Reconsideration Notice. This will include the information you need to be able to appeal.

When and how you you should make another claim to Carer’s Allowance

If the person you look after is awarded one of the appropriate disability benefits, you should make another claim to Carer’s Allowance straight away. If you do not claim Carer’s Allowance within 3 months of the decision on the Disability Living Allowance, Attendance Allowance or Constant Attendance Allowance claim, you may lose benefit.

What to do if the person you look after has claimed a qualifying benefit but is waiting to hear

If the person you look after receives a decision awarding a qualifying benefit you must let us know within 3 months of their decision, or you may lose out on benefit. You can use the form at the end of this letter called Carer’s Allowance reclaim.

If you use this form you may not need to fill in a new Carer’s Allowance claim form, but we may need to ask you for more information. You may be entitled from the date you first claimed or the date the qualifying benefit started.

Further help and advice

You can find more information online at www.gov.uk/carers-allowance.
